Emerging Tech - AI

Artificial Intelligence has changed the landscape of technology and software in the last couple of years - these notes cover a little bit about the history of AI, as well as some basic concepts about what AI is and how it is used by contemporary software engineers.

  • ChatGPT is probably the biggest "name" in the AI world right now, but there are other powerful chatbots being developed, like Google's Bard.
  • Up until the 21st century, there were doubts about the practical and financial applications of AI, so research tended to be a bit slower.
  • Apple acquired Siri in the 2000s, which was one of the first big breakthroughs in mainstream AI's adaptation. Alexa and Google Assistant soon followed.
  • AI has been adopted recently at an extremely accelerated pace, compared to conventional tech adoption.
  • As data volumes online grew and grew in the past decade, it became much more frictionless to actually train generalized (or pseudo-generalized) AI tools.
  • As our hardware has gotten more powerful consistently over time, computers are simply capable of more, faster.
  • The term "Artificial Intelligence" has become a kind of short-hand for computer algorithms that are capable of iterating or "learning" by themselves in order to perform tasks or display "cognitive" abilities typically associated with people.
  • AI is the name for the whole field, while Machine Learning is a subset where algorithms learn from large sets of data. Deep Learning is the field in which algorithms attempt to mimic the behavior of "neural networks" conceptually similar to how a biological brain might think.
  • Chatbots can be a useful shortcut for a lot of things, like roleplaying an interview, quizzing you on certain subjects, asking ChatGPT to summarize certain large blocks of information, etc.
